**Disaster Recovery:**

Disaster Recovery is a set of policies and procedures to enable the recovery or continuation of vital technology infrastructure and systems following a natural or human-induced disaster.

Unlike simple backups, DR focuses on the resumption of services, ensuring that applications, networks, and data are functional within defined timeframes.

**Key Metrics:**

Success in DR is measured by two industry-standard benchmarks:

RTO (Recovery Time Objective): The "Goal Post." How quickly must systems be restored after an outage?

RPO (Recovery Point Objective): The "Safety Net." How much data loss (in time) is the business willing to tolerate?
